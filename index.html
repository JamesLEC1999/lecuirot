About

Clear Thinking in Complex Systems

In safety-critical environments, mistakes are rarely caused by incompetence. They are the predictable result of system design, cognitive load, fatigue, organisational culture, and competing priorities.

I help organisations understand why intelligent, capable people make errors — and how to design systems that are safer, clearer, and more resilient.

With a background in aviation, advanced training in Human Factors, and experience across regulated industries, I specialise in translating complex behavioural science into practical, operational change.

What I do

Human Factors Training

Evidence-based, engaging training designed for real-world application — not tick-box compliance.

Topics include:

  • Fundamentals of Human Factors
  • Non-Technical Skills (NTS)
  • Cognitive Load & Decision-Making
  • Human Error & Just Culture
  • Fatigue & Performance
  • Safety Culture & Reporting Systems

Each session is designed to be practical, scenario-based, and immediately applicable.

Consultancy & Advisory

I work with organisations to:

  • Analyse safety events and uncover systemic contributors
  • Improve reporting and learning systems
  • Strengthen safety culture
  • Design procedures that account for real human behaviour
  • Integrate human factors into governance and policy

My approach combines operational experience with academic rigour — ensuring recommendations are both theoretically sound and practically viable.

Why Human Factors Matters

In aviation, we learned that blaming individuals does not improve safety.

The same principles apply in healthcare, finance, public policy, and emerging technologies.

When systems ignore human limitations:

  • Errors increase
  • Reporting decreases
  • Risk becomes normalised
  • Trust erodes

When systems are designed around how people actually think and perform:

  • Safety improves
  • Performance improves
  • Culture strengthens
  • Organisations become more resilient

About Me

I was born and educated in Jersey before leaving to complete commercial pilot training, qualifying in 2020.

After returning to Jersey, I worked as a pilot while completing a Master’s degree in Human Factors, graduating with Distinction in 2022.

My experience spans aviation, regulatory environments, and safety-critical industries. I am an associate member of the Royal Aeronautical Society, a member of the European Association for Aviation Psychologists, and the International Institute of Risk and Safety Management.

My work focuses on making human factors accessible, practical, and impactful beyond aviation.
