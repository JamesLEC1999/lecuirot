About

I was born and educated in Jersey before leaving to complete commercial pilot training, qualifying in 2020. After returning to Jersey, I worked as a pilot while completing a Master’s degree in Human Factors, graduating with Distinction in 2022. My experience spans aviation, regulatory environments, and safety-critical industries. I am an associate member of the Royal Aeronautical Society, a member of the European Association for Aviation Psychologists, and the International Institute of Risk and Safety Management. My work focuses on making human factors accessible, practical, and impactful beyond aviation.

What I do

I work with organisations to: Analyse safety events and uncover systemic contributors Improve reporting and learning systems Strengthen safety culture Design procedures that account for real human behaviour Integrate human factors into governance and policy My approach combines operational experience with academic rigour — ensuring recommendations are both theoretically sound and practically viable.
